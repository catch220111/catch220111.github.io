Project Overview

Role: Independent Researcher

Location: Beijing, China

Objective: To analyze nozzle performance and thrust output for small-scale solid rocket motors by optimizing geometry.

The Challenge

In small-scale rocketry, maximizing thrust efficiency depends heavily on the internal geometry of the nozzle. The challenge was to determine how varying the exit and entrance angles affects the transition of exhaust gases to supersonic speeds.

Technical Process

  • Numerical Modeling: Developed a custom numerical model using C++ to simulate gas flow and predict thrust based on various geometric parameters.
  • CAD Validation: Utilized CAD modeling to validate the nozzle geometry and ensure the design was feasible for physical construction.
  • Hardware Instrumentation: Constructed a physical load cell thrust measurement device to capture real-time data from test firings.

Value & Findings

The research successfully validated that controlling the nozzle entrance angle serves as an effective primary control for small solid rocket motors.

Key Result: Optimization ensured that exhaust flow consistently reaches Mach speed before reaching the nozzle neck, maximizing the conversion of thermal energy into kinetic energy.
