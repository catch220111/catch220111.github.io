Project Overview

Role: Lead Developer

Tools: Python, NumPy, Matplotlib

Objective: To simulate the gravitational interactions between thousands of stellar bodies to model the dynamics of galactic mergers.

The Challenge

Simulating \(N \) particles requires calculating \(N^2 \) gravitational interactions for every time step. As $N$ increases, the computational cost grows exponentially, making real-time or high-resolution simulations impossible without significant algorithm optimization.

Technical Process

The simulation was built on the fundamental Newtonian law of universal gravitation:

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
  • Algorithm Optimization: Implemented specialized data structures to handle vector calculations more efficiently, resulting in a 15% increase in computation speed compared to standard iterative methods.
  • Physics Integration: Developed a custom time-stepping integrator to ensure orbital stability and energy conservation throughout the collision event.
  • Data Visualization: Utilized Matplotlib to generate visual representations of tidal tails and core mergers, providing a qualitative look at the large-scale structure of the universe.
